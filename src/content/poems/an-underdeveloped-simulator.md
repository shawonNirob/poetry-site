---
title: An Underdeveloped Simulator
date: 2026-05-06
excerpt: A view through a screen, and a command that turns out to be useless.
---
The view is beautiful.
After the rain stops,
everything stands on its own.
Fresh air colors the sky,
guiding the fall of free leaves,
already abandoned by the tree.
On the other side,
there is a screen,
light striking it endlessly.
I live inside that screen.
A scattered moon shines around,
while drowned green leaves show me color,
everything running under my command.
Yet my command is useless,
over those unmoving leaves,
over drops that vanish when the rain stops,
over the heat that must return.
Both views are beautiful, or not.
Perhaps I no longer know
what beauty is.
Perhaps
no beauty exists there to record,
no errors become visible,
nothing that needs fixing.
