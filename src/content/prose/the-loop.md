---
title: The Loop
date: 2026-02-03
excerpt: A puzzle so complete that even the algorithm sent to solve it becomes part of it.
---
Come closer, not to solve, but to stand inside. What one calls a puzzle is not many, nor infinite. It is one number pretending to be countless, a circle mistaken for a road. The beginning touches the ending so gently that neither knows its own name. Those who count say the puzzle never ends. They are mistaken. It ends perfectly, so perfectly that it loops. No mark exists to say where it begins or where it stops. The puzzle is fixed, exact, complete, not because it is infinite, but because it closes upon itself.

Time passes, and an algorithm is proposed to detect the loops. It is instructed to enter the circle and flag the start, then the end. The algorithm proceeds correctly, step by step, obedient and precise. But the moment it enters, the circle absorbs it. The algorithm begins to repeat its own steps, not due to error, but due to correctness. It flags nothing. It becomes the flag.

It becomes the path it was sent to inspect. There is no failure condition, because there is no external position from which failure could be declared. Every observer inside the circle is already part of the mechanism. Thus, the puzzle does not resist solution.

It complies. And compliance is how it keeps going.
