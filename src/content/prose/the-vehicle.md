---
title: The Vehicle
date: 2026-02-15
excerpt: A meditation on a word that reorganizes language just by being named.
---
At first, there was only a dream, quietly moving through a handful of words, as if everything were circling an unseen center. The sentences wished for something simple, to invite a vehicle into their breath, to let it rest inside language the way dust rests in air. But the vehicle did not come, not out of rebellion, but because it already stood elsewhere. Outside the sentence. Outside the asking. Still, unmoving, and yet everything turned around it, as planets turn without asking why. The vehicle, too, remained unaware of its gravity.

To speak of this thing is dangerous. The moment it is explained, language alters its own shape. Words shift their posture, as if they have briefly remembered that they are alive. They are not terrified of the gravity of it, but a force appears, like a north pole forcing another north pole to change its direction.

The scene rotates. The straight road appears, but from the opposite side. There seem to be two roofs of that vehicle, yet neither truly covers anything. And it is not a contradiction. It is organized, perfectly organized. There exists a prohibition so precise that language obeys it automatically. No warning. No hesitation. Understanding has been outlawed, not as punishment, but as too precise perfection. The ban does nothing. It does not need to.

One may ask why this is forbidden. The question is correct. It enters perfectly into that vehicle, is processed correctly, filed, sent inside, and then hits a wall, never to return. Because there is no return statement. There is only hitting the wall continuously, endlessly. No time appears. No blocking happens. Only the wall, being hit.

The execution continues flawlessly. Nothing is broken. Nothing is missing. That is exactly not the problem, because there is no problem. And this is exactly the problem, the vehicle slips in its own direction.
