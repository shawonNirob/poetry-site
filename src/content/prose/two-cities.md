---
title: Two Cities
date: 2026-03-15
excerpt: One city in ruins, one impossibly alive, and a line that runs between them.
---
The war-torn city had lost its pulse. Only destroyed buildings and concrete remained. When a human appears amidst the ruins, he finds a world already dead. No trees remain. Dust coats everything, thickening the air until it becomes a heavy burden to breathe. Nothing moves there except the dust, as though it had been forsaken long ago, abandoned even by the stray dogs.

He sees another city beyond it. That city does not look destroyed, nothing was broken, nothing was out of place. The river flows endlessly. Flowers bloom. The air carries their scent. Children are playing. The sky is blue, and at night the moon shines brightly.

He steps toward the beauty, but as his foot falls, the children stop playing and their bodies become still. The river stops flowing. The trees hide themselves. The sky becomes a vacuum, and the pale moon loses its light. Something inside him stopped moving, as if the city itself stops when he stands there.

He walks backward, but the first city remains destroyed when he crosses back.

At the intersection of the two cities, a line appears. When he stands there, it seems too narrow to stand on. He falls to the left, then rises again, then falls to the right. He remains suspended between them. Yet both cities pull him with exactly the same force, each demanding half of him, as the line separates them so perfectly that nothing could be more precise.

He begins walking along the line. It is not straight, it curves back and joins itself. The line feels familiar, yet he cannot remember why, forming a circle that stretches between the two worlds, where he no longer belongs to either of them.
